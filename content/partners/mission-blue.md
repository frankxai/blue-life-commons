# Partner Profile: Mission Blue (Sylvia Earle Alliance)

> Status: draft, awaiting review. Facts sourced to Mission Blue's own materials and a UN partnership listing (accessed 2026-06-11).

## Who they are

Mission Blue is a marine-conservation nonprofit led by oceanographer Dr. Sylvia Earle, founded following her 2009 TED Prize talk calling for a global network of protected ocean places. It supports a large community-led marine conservation network connecting local ocean champions with global decision-makers through science, advocacy, and action.

## Focus

**Hope Spots** — ecologically critical areas of the ocean championed by local leaders and grounded in science. Mission Blue nominates, supports, and amplifies Hope Spots worldwide, alongside expeditions and storytelling.

## What they offer a commons contributor

- A global, mapped network of Hope Spots that can inform region briefings and partner mapping.
- A model for community-led, locally-championed marine protection that aligns with the commons' region-first approach.
- Public expedition media and educational storytelling (verify licensing before reuse).

## How to engage responsibly

Through Mission Blue's official channels at [missionblue.org](https://missionblue.org/). A commons region briefing that overlaps a Hope Spot should reference it factually; this profile implies no formal partnership unless one is documented.
