# Organization Profile: Olive Ridley Project

> Status: draft, awaiting review. Facts sourced to the organization's own site (accessed 2026-06-11).

## Who they are

The Olive Ridley Project (ORP) is a registered charity founded by Dr. Martin Stelfox in 2013, after he repeatedly encountered olive ridley turtles entangled in ghost nets (abandoned, lost, or discarded fishing gear) while working as a biologist in the Maldives. ORP works to protect sea turtles and their habitats through rescue and conservation medicine, research, and education.

## Focus

Sea turtles — rescue and rehabilitation (including a Marine Turtle Rescue Centre in the Maldives), ghost-net research, photo-ID of individual turtles, and education. Primary regions include the Maldives and the wider Indian Ocean.

## What they offer a commons contributor

- A working model of turtle photo-ID and ghost-net data collection that can inform turtle observation guides and dataset cards.
- Educational material and species expertise relevant to commons turtle pages (verify licensing before reuse).
- A citizen-science pathway: divers and travelers can report turtle sightings and entanglements.

## How to engage responsibly

Through ORP's official channels at [oliveridleyproject.org](https://oliveridleyproject.org/). Any turtle observation guidance in the commons must follow ETHICS.md and ORP's own reporting protocols; do not publish precise nesting-beach locations.
