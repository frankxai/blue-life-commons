# Stranding Response Protocol: <Taxon>

> **The system assembles evidence; it never adjudicates.** This protocol orients trained, authorized responders. It does not authorize anyone to approach, handle, or transport a wild animal. Triage, treatment, and euthanasia are licensed-vet and authority decisions. Read [ETHICS.md](../../ETHICS.md) § Rehabilitation, sanctuary & individual animals.

## First: who to call

The single most important step is **contact the authorized stranding network / hotline** for this region. List it here. Do not collect or transport without authorization.

## Recognize & report

- Species identification cues for <taxon>
- What to report (count, condition, entanglement, behaviour) — **regional location only, never precise coordinates**
- Public-safety + animal-safety cordon basics

## Rapid welfare/triage observations (for authorized responders)

- Body-condition and disturbance signs to record (cited)
- Entanglement / human-interaction indicators (feeds the Human Interaction form)
- Seasonal sensitivities

## Decision pathways (authorized personnel only)

Monitor · refloat/relocate · transport-to-rehab · veterinary assessment — each as *evidence to compile*, not a verdict the document issues. Reference the applicable NOAA Level A / Human Interaction / Rehabilitation Disposition forms.

## Do no harm

This section overrides all others. When in doubt, keep distance, keep the public back, and wait for the authorized network.
