# Local Knowledge: <topic> — <place>

> Preserves the generational, often-oral ecological knowledge held by fishers, divers, and coastal communities — the most perishable and least-substitutable asset a commons can hold. A **commons steward** (not the knowledge-holder) does the Git/PR step; the holder contributes via conversation, voice note, or interview. **Consent and attribution are recorded in frontmatter.**

## The knowledge

State what the holder observed/knows, in their framing, clearly attributed. Distinguish first-hand observation from inherited/traditional knowledge.

## Context

When and where (regional granularity), over what timespan, and how it was learned.

## Corroboration

Where possible, note scientific sources that corroborate or contextualize — **without overriding or "correcting" the holder's knowledge.** Local and scientific knowledge are complementary registers.

## How it can help

What management, monitoring, or education question this knowledge informs (e.g., historical baselines, seasonal patterns, place-based stewardship).
