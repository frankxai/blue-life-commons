# Blue Life Commons Design Contract

## Thesis

The site is an inspectable field library: abyssal context for living systems, warm paper for reading, and exact provenance beside every scientific or media artifact. Beauty earns trust only when the source, rights, review state, and reuse path remain visible.

## First viewport

- Keep the commons proposition and primary contribution path legible in three seconds.
- Use a real approved record as the visual subject, never generated wildlife as scientific proof.
- Pair the animal image with source, license, review, and Git history.
- Preserve quiet space and a clear mobile reading order.

## Materials

- Existing abyss, paper, teal, cyan, kelp, coral, and amber tokens remain canonical.
- Serif communicates editorial care; sans supports navigation; mono carries paths, licenses, source state, and checks.
- Glass is limited to small source/status controls over media.

## Motion

`source-trace` reveals record → evidence → rights → reuse once, then rests. It explains provenance order. Reduced-motion shows all four steps immediately. No ambient animal motion, scroll trapping, or WebGL is justified.

## Asset policy

Tier A approved scientific/institutional media and Tier C exact interface/diagram assets. Generated key art remains concept-only and never serves as identification or conservation evidence.
