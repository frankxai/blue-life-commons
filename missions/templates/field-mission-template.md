# Field Mission Template

> Copy this file to `missions/<region>/<mission-slug>.md`, replace every section, and update the frontmatter. Ethics review (`review.ethics: required`) is mandatory for all field missions.

## Mission overview

What participants will do, observe, and learn — in two or three sentences. Who this is for and what difficulty to expect.

## What you'll need

- Gear list (binoculars, notebook, appropriate clothing…)
- Apps or recording tools, if any
- Permits or access requirements

## Where and when

Regional guidance only — **never publish precise locations of vulnerable populations** (ETHICS.md). Note the best seasons and times of day, and seasonal sensitivities to avoid (breeding, pupping, nesting, molting).

## Ethical observation rules (do no harm)

This section overrides everything else in the mission.

- Minimum approach distance: state it, with the regulation or guideline source
- Signs of disturbance for the target species, with sources — and the rule: **if you see them, you are too close; leave**
- Never touch, feed, bait, chase, or surround animals
- Local regulations: cite them

## Mission steps

1. Step-by-step instructions a beginner can follow
2. What to observe and how to record it
3. How to wrap up and leave the site undisturbed

## Recording your observations

What data to capture (species, count, behavior, conditions, date/time, regional location) and where to submit it (e.g., a recognized citizen-science platform for your region).

## What your observations contribute to

Connect the mission to research, conservation, or education outcomes — with sources.
