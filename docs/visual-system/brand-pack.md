# Blue Life Commons Brand Pack

## Role

Blue Life Commons creates trust. It is the free public-good knowledge layer of ocean intelligence: species pages, region briefings, field missions, dataset cards, sources, ethics, and review.

Ocean Intelligence System creates continuity. It is the software layer: connectors, guardians, dashboards, alerts, and provenance-preserving signals.

Starlight Marine Intelligence Systems creates reach. It is the implementation layer: design, institutional adoption, media, reliability, and partner delivery.

## Personality

Clear, warm, practical, and humble about science. The brand should feel like a capable field station with a generous public library attached.

Use:

- plain-language confidence
- source-led calm
- marine warmth without sentimentality
- care for animals without anthropomorphic claims
- practical usefulness over vague inspiration

Avoid:

- guilt-heavy "save the ocean" language
- mystical animal translation
- glamorous close-contact wildlife imagery
- generic blue tech gradients
- fake dashboards, fake maps, or fake conservation numbers

## Typography

Use Instrument Sans or Geist for all exact assets. Use Geist Mono only for source IDs, connector names, and metadata snippets.

Do not bake small text into generated images. If a graphic needs text, compose it as SVG/code or overlay it after generation.

## Logo Concept

The primary mark is a **review card with a living current**:

- The card represents a source-led public artifact.
- The current represents ocean life and knowledge moving through the commons.
- The warm node represents review, stewardship, and the human handoff.
- The open frame avoids a closed institutional seal; the commons stays accessible.

The logo intentionally avoids whale tails, anchors, globes, generic orbit rings, and node-cluster AI marks.

## Grok Generation Rules

Use Grok for emotional, inspectable, non-factual imagery:

- animal in habitat, no exact locations
- human stewardship at respectful distance
- field station, notebooks, public workshops
- underwater scenes with no text or claims
- hero backgrounds and social crops

Do not use Grok for:

- exact species identification plates
- charts, labels, dashboards, text-heavy infographics
- legal/safety guidance
- medical, rescue, or clinical decisions
- animal distress imagery unless there is a specific reviewed educational need

## QA Bar

Ship visual assets only when they pass:

- 26/30 or higher on the generated asset quality gate
- no animal-safety red flags
- no garbled text or fake labels
- correct crop for the target surface
- source/provenance recorded
- exact-text overlays composed outside the image model
