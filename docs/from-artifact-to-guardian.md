# From Artifact to Guardian

Blue Life Commons is the knowledge. The [Ocean Intelligence System](https://github.com/frankxai/ocean-intelligence-system) is the machinery that puts that knowledge to work as living agents — **Ocean Guardians** that watch over a reef, a bay, a species, or a coastline.

This is how a reviewed commons artifact becomes a guardian people can rely on.

## The path

```
Reviewed commons artifact (species page · region briefing · dataset card)
        │  grounds
        ▼
Ocean Guardian (place-scoped agent in ocean-intelligence-system)
        │  queries (read-only, provenance preserved)
        ▼
Real marine data via connectors (OBIS, Coral Reef Watch, Protected Planet, …)
        │  produces
        ▼
Briefings · alerts · dashboards for NGOs, researchers, creators, coastal residents
```

## Why the commons comes first

A guardian is only as trustworthy as its grounding. The Ocean Intelligence System enforces a rule it inherits from this repository: **grounded or silent.** A guardian states a fact only if that fact traces to a reviewed commons artifact or a cited Tier 1–2 source. So a guardian for a place cannot exist until the commons has:

- a **region briefing** for that place,
- **species pages** for its flagship and indicator species, and
- **dataset cards** for the data the guardian will read.

That is why contributing to the commons *is* building the guardian. The page you write today is the spine of the agent that watches that water tomorrow.

## The ethics inheritance

Every guardian inherits this repository's [`ETHICS.md`](../ETHICS.md) as an upstream, non-waivable standard. Guardians never publish precise locations of vulnerable populations, and never generate wildlife-approach guidance dynamically — they serve the reviewed observation guide instead. The Ocean Intelligence System cannot relax these rules; it can only honor them.

## What this means for you

| You are | Your path |
|---|---|
| A contributor | Write the species page or region briefing. It becomes guardian grounding. |
| An NGO | Your reviewed artifacts become guardians that brief your team and supporters. |
| A developer | Build a connector (in the Ocean Intelligence System) and register its `dataset-card` here. |
| Someone who lives by the sea | Ask for a guardian for your water. The first step is grounding it — a briefing for your region. |

Knowledge here. Machinery there. Ethics upstream of both.
