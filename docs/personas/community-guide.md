# Guide for Ocean Communities

For coastal residents, fishers, divers, surfers, and anyone who lives alongside the ocean — this commons belongs to you as much as to researchers. Here is how to use it and contribute to it.

---

## Subscribe to Local Ocean Alerts

Guardian agents in the Ocean Intelligence System watch over named ocean regions — Great Barrier Reef, Salish Sea, Monterey Bay, Galápagos, and more — and emit alerts when something measurable changes: a coral bleaching event escalates, an unusual species absence is detected, water temperature crosses a threshold.

**Subscribe to alerts for your region:**

1. Visit the [Ocean Intelligence System dashboard](https://github.com/frankxai/ocean-intelligence-system) (dashboard link in the OIS README)
2. Select your region from the Guardian map
3. Choose your alert threshold: informational · moderate · high · critical
4. Enter your email or webhook endpoint

Alerts arrive as plain-language summaries with source citations and a link to the underlying data. No technical knowledge required.

## How to Report a Stranded Marine Animal

If you encounter a stranded marine animal (dolphin, whale, seal, sea lion, turtle), your immediate report can save its life. Do not touch or approach the animal — call first.

**By region — primary contacts:**

| Region | Organization | Contact |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| USA (Pacific) | The Marine Mammal Center | (415) 289-SEAL |
| USA (Atlantic / Gulf) | NOAA Marine Mammal Stranding Network | 1-866-755-6622 |
| Australia | ORRCA (NSW) / DBCA (WA) | 02 9415 3333 |
| UK | British Divers Marine Life Rescue | 01825 765546 |
| Global (24h lookup) | IFAW Stranding Network | ifaw.org/stranding |

After you've called, document what you can from a safe distance: species (or best guess), condition, location coordinates, estimated size, and any visible injuries. This information is invaluable to responders.

**Contributing your observation to BLC:** Once the stranding event is resolved, you can contribute a field observation via iNaturalist (linked to our Stranding-Network Guardian). Instructions in the citizen science section below.

## Sustainable Fishing Guidance

The sustainable practices library at [`content/practices/`](../../content/practices/) covers evidence-graded guidance for:

- **Fisheries:** gear type selection by target species, bycatch reduction techniques, seasonal restrictions rationale, catch-and-release best practices
- **Aquaculture:** site selection criteria, species suitability, feed sourcing standards
- **Coastal tourism:** dive and snorkel interaction guidelines, wildlife watching distance rules, anchor-free mooring options

Each practice entry includes the evidence grade (A = strong RCT/meta-analysis evidence; B = observational studies; C = expert consensus), source citations, and applicable regions. Written for practitioners, not academics.

## Contribute a Citizen Science Observation

Your eyes on the water are irreplaceable data. Here are the best ways to contribute:

**iNaturalist** — the fastest path. Any ocean observation with a photo gets automatically linked to the OBIS connector, which feeds directly into Guardian agents. Create an account at [inaturalist.org](https://www.inaturalist.org) and join the **Blue Life Commons** project to have your observations flow directly into our monitoring system.

**eBird / OBIS mobile** — for seabird and marine species sightings with GPS coordinates.

**Reef Check** — for coral reef health surveys. Reef Check trains citizen scientists to conduct standardized surveys using the same protocol as professional reef ecologists. Join a survey or start a new site: [reefcheck.org](https://www.reefcheck.org).

**PADI Dive Against Debris** — report marine debris during any dive using the PADI app. Data flows into coastal health assessments. [padi.com/courses/dive-against-debris](https://www.padi.com/courses/dive-against-debris).

## Local Ocean Health Dashboard

Each of the 8 regional briefings in BLC is paired with a live Guardian agent that maintains a current-state dashboard: temperature trend, recent species observations, bleaching status (for reef regions), and any active alerts.

Access regional dashboards via the OIS gateway or the links in each regional briefing at [`content/regions/`](../../content/regions/).

## The Commons Hard Rule

> Every conversation becomes an issue. Every issue becomes an artifact. Every artifact becomes public knowledge, partner leverage, or funded impact.

If you know something about your local ocean that is not yet in these pages — a species behavior observation, a local fishing practice, a community monitoring effort — open an issue. We will work with you to turn it into a sourced, credited artifact that benefits the whole community.

Issues: [github.com/frankxai/blue-life-commons/issues](https://github.com/frankxai/blue-life-commons/issues)
